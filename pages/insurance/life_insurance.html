Life Insurance

In most cases, life insurance provides a promised amount of funds to the individuals(beneficaries) selected by the policy holder after the latter's death in exchange for a periodic premium. In the case of an endownment policy, money is paid to the policy holder if he/she is alive after a specified date.

  • The two types of insurance companies are "stock"(investor-owned) and "mutual"(policyholder-owned) life insurance companies.
    • 85% are stock; 15% are mutual
    • Stock life insurance companies sell nonparticipating/nonpar policies
    • Mutual life insurance companies sell participating/par policies that have a higher premium than their counterpart, but refund part of the premium annually through the policy dividend

  • Life insurance has two major types: temporary/"term" or permanent with subcategories/options for both.
    • Temporary/term insurance is life insurance protection for a specified period of time;
      • Renewability Option: can be renewed after end of specified term(5/10 years periods) for a higher premium that will be stable for duration of next period
      • Straight Term: pay same premium for entire life of the policy
      • Conversion Option: can be exchanged/shifted to a permanent life policy without a medical examination for a higher premium
      • Decreasing term: premium stays sames for lifetime of insurance in exchange for smaller payouts to beneficaries overtime
      • Return of Premium(ROP)/money-back: will return the money of policy in full if end term of policy is reached; usually more expensive than regular policy
      • Re-entry term: renewable policy for lower premiums if overall health is maintained

    • Permanent/lifetime life insurance
      • Whole life: policyholder pays a set premium each year for as long as he or she lives; a.k.a. straight/cash-value life policy
      • Limited Payment: pay premium for a limited period before being insured for rest of life
      • Modified: lower initial premiums for set period(5-10 years) before major increase for rest of policy
      • Adjustable: changable/flexible life policy based on needs
      • Universal: combines term insurance and investment elements

  • Group Life Insurance Policy: insures a group of people under a single policy that does not require medical examination

  • Endownment Life Insurance Policy: promises payment to policy holder even if they are alive at end of policy term
  • Payment-related provisions
    • Policy Reinstatement: a lapsed policy can be put back if not converted to cash given certain conditions
    • Grace Period: an allowed period(29-31 days usually) within a time frame where you can pay the premium without penalty or the policy lapsing
    • Automatic Premium Loan: the insurance company may automatically take money out of the policy's cash value if you do not pay the premium within the grace period
    • Guranteed Insurability Option: allows you to buy addition life insurance at stated intervals without proof of insurability

  • Common Policy Clauses
    • Nonforfeiture Clause: allows the insured not to forfeit all accrued benefits if you drop the policy
    • incontestability Clause: states that the insurer(insurance company) cannot dispute the validity of a policy for the lifetime of the insured after a specified period (1-2 years in the policy).
    • Suicide Clause: states that if the insured dies by suicide during the first two years the policy is in force, the death benefit will ONLY equal the amount of the premium paid.
