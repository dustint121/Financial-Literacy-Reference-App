Health Insurance

Health insurance provides protection to alleviate the financial burdens of individuals sufferring from illness or injury.

Health insurance is sold as plan of a group plan or an individual plan.

  • Group Plan
    • 90% of all health insurance plans
    • Most plans are employer or organization(labor unions) sponsored
    • The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017 requires employers (>50 employees) to provide health insurance to employees
    • Most group plans have a coordination of benefits(COB) provision to combine multiple plans (if you have them) and prevent you getting more than 100% coverage of your allowed medical needs.

  • Individual Plan
    • Covers either just you or your family
    • Allows room to tailor the plan to your specific needs
    • Suicide Clause: states that if the insured dies by suicide during the first two years the policy is in force, the death benefit will ONLY equal the amount of the premium paid.

This is a list of the major categories that medical insurance can cover.

  • Basic categories
    • Hospital Expense Insurance: pays part or all of hospital bills for room, board, and other charges
    • Surgical Expense Insurance: pays part or all of the surgeon's fees for an operation
    • Physician Expense Insurance: provides benefits for doctors' fees for nonsurgical care, x-rays, and lab tests.
    • Dental Expense Insurance: usually includes a deductible and coinsurance provision
    • Vision Expense Insurance
  • Comprehensive categories
    • Basic Health Insurance Coverage: combination of hospital expense insurance, surgical expense insurance, and physician expense insurance
    • Major Medical Expense Insurance: pays most of the costs exceeding those covered by the hospital, surgical, and physician expense policies.
      • has a deductible provision that specifies an minimum the insured must pay before benefits become payable by the insurance company.
      • has a coinsurance provision under which the policy holder must pay a specified percentage of the health bill after the deduction before the rest is covered by the insurer
    • Hospital Indemnity Policy: pays stipulated daily, weekly, or monthly cash benefits during hospital confinement
    • Long-term Care Insurance(LTC): pays for the cost of day-in, day-out care for long-term illness or disability

This is a list of the major medical insurance provisions found in most policies.

  • Eligibility
  • Assigned Benefits: who gets the insurance payments(you or your doctor)
  • Internal Limits: fixed amount of payment for hospital stays
  • Copayment: a provision where the insured pays a flat amount for each medical service payment after the deductible has been met
  • Service Benefits: gurantee full coverage to specific services, regardless of costs
  • Coordination of Benefits
  • Guaranteed Renewable: insurer cannot cancel policy unless you fail to pay premiums on time
  • Cancellation and Termiation: the specific situations where the insurer can cancel your policy

Health insurance is sold as plan of a group plan or an individual plan.

  • Medicare
    • a federal health insurance program for people 65 or older, people of any age with permanent kidney failure, and people with certain disabilities.
    • has hospital insurance to cover hospital care
    • has mdeical insurance to pay for doctors' and other services
    • can pay for prescription drugs

  • Medicaid
    • a program of medical assistance to low-income individuals and families.
    • each state has its specific implementation of Medicaid (e.g. Medi-Cal is California's implementation of Medicaid)
