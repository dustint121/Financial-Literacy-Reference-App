Insurance Basics

Insurance is protection against possible financial loss. The system of insurance works due to the principle of pooling risk, where multiple people add to a "pool" of funds with the expectation that only a fraction of the contributing individuals will actually suffer a loss and take part of the pool.

  • Insurance company/insurer: a risk-sharing group that agrees to assume financial responsibility for losses that may result from an insured risk
  • Policy: the contract from the insurer that an individual signs to take part risk-sharing group
  • Policyholder/Insured: the person(you) who signs the policy to get insurance
  • Premium: the fee a policyholder pays periodically(monthly/annually) to keep the policy
  • Liability: the legal responsibility for the financial cost of another person's losses or injuries
  • Risk: the uncertainty or lack of predictability of an event
  • Peril: the cause of a possible loss(e.g. fire, robbery, robber, death, etc.)
  • Hazard: something that increases the likelihood of loss through some peril
  • Risk Management Methods
    • Risk avoidance: not participating in activities that have said risk(e.g. driving, working in risky environment); the most extreme option
    • Risk reduction: taking actions that reduce risk in activity(e.g. seatbelt or safety precautions)
    • Risk shifting: transfer risk to another individual/group(e.g. insurance)
    • Risk assumption: taking responsibility for possible loss or injury from risk; a.k.a. no insurance/protection
